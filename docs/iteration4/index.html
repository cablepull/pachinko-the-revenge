How to play

If you've never seen a Japanese pachinko machine, here's everything you need.

The premise

You're sitting at a digital pachinko cabinet. Hold the launch knob on the right (SPACE) — the machine fires steel balls up and over the playfield. They fall through a field of pins, bouncing unpredictably, and either land in the central chucker (the gold cup labelled HESO) or drop off the bottom. Only a ball entering the chucker triggers a reel spin. Match figures across the reels and you hit a jackpot — the attacker doors open and ~1,440 balls pour out.

The base game returns ~25–40 balls per 100 fired (pin layout is the calibration). The texture is in between: each spin can escalate into a reach — an animation that telegraphs "this might be the one" — and the longer/louder the reach, the higher the chance the reels actually line up.

New in iteration 4: the cabinet now has a depth-axis player input. The headline mechanic is 釘調整 (nail-adjustment) — between sessions (and at chapter 2+) you can press T to open the tuning workshop, drag pin-cluster knobs, and see the predicted ベース as an honest confidence interval (e.g., "17.4% ± 3.2%"). Your tuning persists across sessions. The default canvas is contextually minimal: the cosmetic streak multiplier is gone, the persistent P/L strip was replaced with floaters off the chucker, and HUDs are opt-in. A session-end summary screen appears on R with your duration, balls fired/won, longest dry streak, rarest reach, and narrative lines. Returning within 7 days shows a "welcome back" card with your prior tuning. Driven by audit-002 + new constraints C-19 (釘調整) and C-20 (contextual minimalism).

What you'll see on screen

  • KNOB (right) the launch dial. Hold SPACE/mouse to fire balls. Pointer rotates while engaged.
  • PIN FIELD ~80 steel pins in a diamond pattern. Balls bounce off them — small random kicks decorrelate identical paths.
  • CHUCKER (HESO) the gold cup. Catching a ball here is the ONLY way to trigger a reel spin in v0.2.
  • 3 reels (LCD) result is the middle row. Three matching figures = jackpot. Three matching 7s = FEVER.
  • REACH banners a spin escalated. Calm (~2% hit), mid (~25%), premium (~70%), confirmed (~95%).
  • ATTACKER opens during jackpots. ~1,440 balls per 16-round jackpot.
  • KAKUHEN 165-spin chance time after most jackpots. Rate jumps from 1/200 to 1/36. Cabinet turns red.
  • DATA LAMP top-right HUD: spins-since-JP, balls FIRED/RETURNED/RATE, JACKPOTS, BALLS WON, last 10 JP gaps.
  • CHAPTER revenge plot advances with each jackpot. New chapter = new reach animations unlocked.

The math (real spec sheet)

  • Base jackpot 1 / 199.8 per spin
  • Kakuhen jackpot 1 / 35.9 per spin
  • Kakuhen entry 70% of jackpots enter kakuhen
  • ST window 165 spins, deterministic
  • Payout 16 rounds × ~90 balls = ~1,440 per jackpot

What you should be doing

Hold SPACE to keep balls flowing. Watch the FIRED / RETURNED / RATE counter — that's the only feedback on how the pin layout is treating you. Aim for reaches. Get to kakuhen. Chain jackpots inside the 165-spin window. Unlock all four chapters.

There is no money, no leaderboard, no save-across-sessions yet. This is a single-cabinet study in pachinko grammar. Press H to bring this panel back any time.
